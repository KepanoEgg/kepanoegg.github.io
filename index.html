I created this document from a combination of wiki.dothraki.org (which takes 20 seconds to a minute to load a page), some [blog posts](http://www.dothraki.com/) directly from David J. Peterson (the creator of the non-common languages), and a small handful of other resources I've found on the internet. wiki.dothraki often cites the lines as they were intended either from the books, or how they were written by DJP, so this document will not match what was originally subtitled in the show. I apologize for any typos, or mistakes, as I'm bastardizing many other peoples' work. I did my best to seperate scenes based on the context of the dialogue and my rough memory of what was happening in the episode. I have cut out the original language transcripts, a lot of info about the subtleties of the language, and the production notes about why the language was spoken a certain way, or when someone misspoke because they weren't a native speaker. I have only left in a handful of notes where it may be confusing, or look like a mis-translation otherwise. If you would like more info on the lines, or to read the words in their spoken language, I would refer you back to wiki.dothraki.org. As far as I can tell there are 4 distinct languages/dialects that should be addressed, and I'll do my best to show them as they are spoken, without interrupting the flow of this document. A fifth language is a single line, and knowing the speaker is a spoiler so I won't reveal it until it is spoken.
